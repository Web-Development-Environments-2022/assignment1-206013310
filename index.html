about me
" I was born in Debrecen (Hungary) in 1926, to Sara and Mordechai David Gutman.
When the war began we were sent to a labor camp, and luckily we all survived.
I moved alone to Israel in 1946, after I joined to "Hashomer Hatzair".
I met my husband Shmuel Sami Kramer here in Israel and in 1960 I married him.
Our first son, Amos, was born in 1961, and our second son, Amir, was born in 1964.
Throughout the years my granddaughters Oren, Shahar and Tomer were born (Amos's daughters).
I'm working as a seamstress, but my real passion is sculpting and poetry.
I have even published a poems book called "Autumn buds" (in hebrew "Nitzaney Stav").
Nature is what brings me the most joy, and it is reflected in my art works and my poems. "

Lea passed away in 2015.
R.I.P

Q1
Web 1 Web 2 Web 3
Web1 Mostly based on HTML/Portals. Sites are static, and you must physically update the page whenever you want to make changes (not dynamic- read only). The design is very simple and sparse, and it is not interactive. Advertisements on websites while surfing the internet are banned. Furthermore, in order to access a specific page, the address must be known in advance. More dynamic (read and write). Users could add their own content and could set up websites. As a result, the level of web design has risen and become more interactive. Tag language has also been developed to communicate information between sites, which will link more in terms of meaning and semantics and less in HTML. Also called "The semantic web", improves web technologies in demand to create, share and connect content through search and analysis based on the capability to comprehend the meaning of words, rather than on keywords or numbers. Combining AI capability with NLP. Furthermore, the three-dimensional design is being used widely in websites and services in Web 3.0.
